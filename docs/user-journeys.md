# How it works

This page describes who uses the registry and what they can do.

---

## Who uses this platform

### Breeders and kennel owners

Breed dogs professionally or semi-professionally. Registered with a kennel club
or breed-specific organisation. Track bloodlines carefully and rely on the
registry as an authoritative record.

### Working dog owners and club members

Own one or several dogs. Compete in field tests, breed surveys, or shows. Want
to record achievements and prove their dogs' lineage is clean. Need the registry
to work well on mobile when out in the field.

### Researchers and prospective buyers

Research bloodlines before buying a puppy or selecting a stud. Look up kennels,
check test results, view pedigrees. May have an account but haven't submitted
any dogs themselves.

### Club administrators

Manage the registry on behalf of the breed club. Approve new members, review dog
submissions, manage qualifications. Power users who value efficiency and minimal
clicks.

---

## Key concepts

### Dog display name

Every dog's **display name** includes their registered name plus earned qualification
prefixes. For example, a dog named "Kennel von Haus Example" with approved VGP and
HZP qualifications appears as "VGP-HZP Kennel von Haus Example" everywhere in the
registry.

- Only approved qualifications contribute to the prefix
- Only qualifications marked as "adds to name" contribute
- The registered name in the database is never changed

### Breeder visibility

Each kennel decides whether its breeders are listed publicly. When visible,
breeders appear in the dropdown when registering dogs bred at that kennel.
When hidden, submitters select "Other / not listed" and the kennel is notified
to link the correct breeder.

### Health records

Health tests are mandatory on all dog submissions. For each standard test (like
Hips or Eyes), you must either enter a result or explicitly select "Not tested".
Leaving health fields blank is not permitted.

### Certificates

Certificates uploaded with qualification results are used for admin verification
only. They are never shown publicly on dog profiles.

## Creating an account

1. Visit the registry and click **Sign in**.
2. Create an account with email or Google.
3. You'll see a **pending** page explaining that your account awaits approval.
4. An admin reviews your registration (typically 1-2 business days).
5. Once approved, sign in normally and access the full registry.

---

## Submitting a dog

1. Click **+ Add dog** in the navigation bar.
2. Fill in the form sections:
   - **Identity**: name, sex, breed
   - **Details**: date of birth, colour, chip ID, registration number
   - **Birth kennel and breeder**: search or create new
   - **Parentage**: search for sire/dam or enter free text
   - **Health records**: required — enter results or select "Not tested"
   - **Photo**: upload (optional)
   - **Qualifications**: add any known qualifications (optional)
   - **Clubs**: membership in clubs and organisations (optional)
3. Click **Submit**.
4. Your dog is created with pending status and goes to the approval queue.
5. Once approved, the dog appears in search results.

---

## Looking up dogs and kennels

The search page starts empty by design. Type a name, chip ID, or registration
number to search. Filter by breed and sex.

Dog profiles show the display name (with qualification prefixes) as the main
heading. Qualifications are displayed near the top, grouped by organisation.
Health records are mandatory and always shown.

Kennel profiles show contact details, breeders (if publicly listed), and dogs
associated with the kennel.

---

## Adding a qualification result

1. Open a dog's profile page.
2. Click **Add qualification result**.
3. Select the qualification from the dropdown (grouped by organisation).
4. Fill in the result fields (score, grade, or pass/fail depending on the type).
5. Upload a certificate (optional now, required before approval).
6. Submit.

The result is pending until an admin approves it. Once approved, the dog's
display name updates if the qualification adds a prefix.

---

## The approval queue (for admins)

All submissions go through an approval queue with five tabs:

- **Users**: new account registrations
- **Dogs**: dog submissions
- **Kennels**: kennel edits
- **Qualifications**: qualification results (with certificate preview)
- **Ownership**: ownership transfer requests

Admins can approve or reject each item. Certificates are viewable in the queue
only — they're never shown publicly.

---

## Key principles

1. **Status is always visible** — pending, approved, or rejected with context.
2. **Display name includes qualifications** — computed automatically, shown everywhere.
3. **Free text fallback always** — parentage and breeders can be entered as text if not in the registry.
4. **Health records are mandatory** — every test needs a result or "Not tested".
5. **Certificates are admin-only** — used for verification, never shown publicly.
6. **Search is intentional** — the page is empty until you type (by design).
